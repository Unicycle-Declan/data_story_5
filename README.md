# Abstract

This study evaluates restoration efforts in Compartment 60 on the Domain of the University of the South in Sewanee, Tennessee, focusing on hill cane (_Arundinaria appalachiana_) and planted shortleaf pine (_Pinus echinata_). The site was shelterwood harvested in 2019, with prescribed burning applied in surrounding areas in 2025 to promote regeneration. Data were collected on overstory structure including tree height, basal area, and trees per acre, along with hill cane coverage and clump size and shortleaf pine growth. Correlation analyses showed that overstory variables had little to no relationship with hill cane coverage, indicating that canopy manipulation did not significantly influence its success. Observations suggested that hill cane may be more affected by soil and moisture conditions rather than overstory conditions. In contrast, shortleaf pine showed strong early survival and growth following planting. Overall, restoration efforts were unsuccessful at promoting hill cane through overstory manipulation but have been successful so far for shortleaf pine establishment.
